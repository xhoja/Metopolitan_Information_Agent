## Mission & Objectives

Build a full-stack, AI-powered university management system that gives students instant personalised academic guidance, provides administrators unified control over users and data, and gives professors a single platform to manage courses, grades, attendance, and assignments.

**Objectives:**
- Deliver a working web application with three role-based portals (Admin, Professor, Student)
- Integrate a conversational AI adviser (M.I.A) accessible only to students
- Maintain a clean, documented codebase on GitHub throughout the project
- Meet all course milestone deadlines

## Role Responsibilities

### Captain
Keeps team on track. Owns the sprint plan. Escalates blockers. Chairs weekly meetings.

### Editor
Owns all written deliverables — wiki pages, guidelines, meeting reports. Reviews docs before submission.

### Client Rep
Primary point of contact with the client. Attends all client meetings. Documents requirements and feedback.

### Tester
Owns the QA process. Tests features before merge. Documents bugs and regression issues.

### Researcher
Leads technology evaluation. Builds proof-of-concept "Hello World" apps. Documents findings in wiki.

### Repo Master
Owns the GitHub repository. Enforces branch policy (no direct commits to `main`). Reviews and merges PRs.

### Optimist
Advocates for what is achievable. Generates ideas. Keeps team morale high when progress is slow.

### Pessimist
Devil's advocate. Identifies risks, edge cases, and failure modes before they become problems.

### Analyst
Owns requirements analysis. Translates client needs into user stories and acceptance criteria.

## Workflow & Contribution Guidelines

1. All work happens on feature branches — `feature/your-feature` or `fix/your-fix`
2. No direct commits to `main`
3. Every PR requires at least one team member review before merge
4. Commit messages follow Conventional Commits: `feat:`, `fix:`, `docs:`, `chore:`
5. Weekly log entries must be posted to the wiki before each team meeting

---

## Conflict Resolution Process

1. **Raise it early** — If a disagreement arises, surface it at the next team meeting, not over text.
2. **Each side states their position** — Two minutes each, no interruptions.
3. **Team votes** — Majority rules on technical or process disagreements.
4. **Captain decides** — If the vote is tied, the Captain makes the final call.
5. **Escalate** — If the conflict is unresolved after two meetings, escalate to the course instructor.
6. **Document the outcome** — Record the decision in the meeting report for that week.
